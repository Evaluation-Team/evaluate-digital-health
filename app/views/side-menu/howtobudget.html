Budget for evaluation

While it’s recommended that you allocate 10% of your total project delivery budget to evaluation, this may not be feasible or adequate for your project. Your evaluation budget should be proportionate to what you need to learn from your evaluation.

Scope of your evaluation

You need to consider how extensive your evaluation should be. Conducting a robust evaluation is more cost-effective than having to repeat an evaluation because it was not adequately resourced. If you carry out a poorly resourced evaluation, you may:

  • be unable to determine whether or not the project has had a positive health outcome
  • inadvertently continue with a project which has negligible or even negative impact
  • unintentionally continue with a project that is not cost-effective

Setting up your evaluation

The key steps to setting up an evaluation are:

  • define your health product’s intended outcomes
  • choose your success indicators
  • select appropriate methods

Depending on which evaluation methods you choose, you’ll need to recruit participants to take part in your evaluation.

Carrying out the evaluation

To successfully evaluate your health product, you’ll require a multidisciplinary team to:

  • carry out the evaluation including data collection, analysis, sourcing tools and using data sets
  • manage relationships with internal colleagues and external organisations
  • provide guidance

Analysing and sharing your findings

You must record the findings of your evaluation before communicating them to the relevant people.

You should also use your findings to improve the design of your health product.
