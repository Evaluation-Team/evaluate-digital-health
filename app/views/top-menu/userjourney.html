Map your user journey

A user journey is a simple timeline from left to right, showing how your users interact with your health product at different stages. You can then link these interactions to the health outcomes of your health product.

Health outcomes are changes that are intentionally produced by a health product to improve a user’s health.

How a user journey supports your evaluation

Develop a user journey to get an overview of how your health product meets user needs and achieves its intended health outcomes over time. Creating a user journey will help you align your user needs and how they interact with your product with your intended health outcomes.

Case study: Couch to 5K

The Couch to 5K team started by considering the different stages in their users’ relationship with the app – from being completely unaware of it to where they stop using it, and the important moments in between.

This helped them think about how the app's activities meet the users’ needs, and how they could achieve their intended health outcomes.

For example, the team know their users need to stay motivated when using the app. To meet this need, the app provides motivational messages and bonus incentives. This targets the health outcome 'the user is confident enough to go for a run'.

How to map your user journey

Step 1: Map the user’s journey through your service

You can create a user journey for an existing health product, or for an entirely new one. You can create a user journey during a face-to-face or online workshop.

The evaluation lead for your health product or another team member should facilitate the user journey workshop and ensure the outputs are written up and shared with the team.

Your research should help you understand who your users are, their needs and how they could potentially use your health product.

You should work with your stakeholders to understand the different stages of your user journey, and which user needs are met at each stage. As you do this, map the relevant user needs to the appropriate stage.

This activity will help you understand the health product from the user’s point of view, and establish the most important touchpoints in their journey.

Step 2: Map the health outcomes to the user journey

As you develop your health product, you will need to reach a clear understanding of your service’s actions, or what your product does for the user, at each stage of the user journey.

Work with your team to bring together all the information you have about what the product does and map these actions to the user journey.

Step 3: Map the service actions to the user journey

Once you know the health outcomes of your health product, you can start thinking about how these relate to the user and the user journey.

You then need to map the short, medium and long-term health outcomes from your logic model to the user journey.

Don’t worry if you cannot map all of the health outcomes. Some medium and long-term health outcomes, such as ‘reduction in cardiovascular diseases in the United Kingdom’, will not be measurable during the product journey.
