Choose evaluation methods

An evaluation method is a way of collecting data on the impact of a digital health intervention. It is useful to select the evaluation methods that are most appropriate to your indicators and outcomes, so you can collect the relevant data.

Case study: Couch to 5K

The Couch to 5K team completed their logic model, mapped their service and chose their indicators, so they had everything they needed to choose their evaluation methods.

They used the methods library filters to understand which methods might be suitable for their project.

The team did not have the resources to conduct a full randomised control trial, but the methods library showed that a series of A/B tests may be the best way to evaluate the service.


How to choose your methods

The National Institute for Health and Care Excellence have put together a set of evidence standards for digital health technologies that are intended to be commissioned or used within the UK health and social care system.

Public Health England has created a library of methods, were you can use filters to understand which methods will be most useful in evaluating your digital health intervention.
