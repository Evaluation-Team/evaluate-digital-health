Prepare for evaluation

Evaluation is essential to finding out what works and what does not work.

You need to decide on the amount of budget, people and time you can dedicate to evaluating your health product. It’s important that you evaluate your health product so you can improve it – but the scope of your evaluation will depend on certain conditions. For example, you should consider:

  • carrying out an in-depth evaluation if your product is of high political scrutiny
  • conducting a lighter evaluation if your product is based on existing evidence

Evaluating new health products

It’s particularly important to conduct an evaluation for a new health product when one or more of the following criteria are met because:

  • there has been a significant investment of time, budget and resources
  • there’s a possibility of risk or harm to the user’s health or wellbeing
  • the health product is the subject of high political scrutiny
  • there’s a gap in services or knowledge about how to address a problem or provide effective services for a particular population

Evaluating existing health products

If you’re aiming to improve an existing health product, you need to carry out a formative evaluation. This involves continuously gaining feedback and improving your product based on the feedback you receive from your users.
